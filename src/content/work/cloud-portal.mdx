<CaseSection id="context">

<CaseSubsection title="Suite in sales, bag of apps in product">

After six acquisitions, Sitecore’s product organization was fragmented, <a href="/work/blok">as described in the Blok case study</a>. Yet to customers, Sitecore was positioned as a unified **digital experience platform**.

Buyers expect multi-product suites to behave like Adobe or Microsoft: **one front door, one identity, seamless switching**.

My internal framing of the challenge:

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="problem">

<CaseSubsection title="The platform was homeless">

Customers experienced the **opposite of a platform**: separate logins, choppy workflows between apps, and no way to discover sibling products.

Without a shared entry point, and without easy switching between apps, the platform story did not match the product reality.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="approach">

<CaseSubsection title="Secure the mandate">

I pitched a quick concept for a **unified portal and app launcher**, with an **app switcher** that would be embedded inside each product:

<img src="/work/portal-concept.png" alt="Rapid concept for a unified portal and app switcher" className="w-full rounded-lg border shadow-lg my-10" />

This got me the role of Design Lead on what would become the new **Cloud Portal**.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Two birds, one stone">

Since I was the lead designer on both the Cloud Portal and the <a href="/work/blok">new design system</a>, this put me at a unique advantage. The Cloud Portal was simultaneously the first testing ground for the new design system, and its product needs fed back into the design system.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Course correction">

Several months into development, **feature creep** was putting the success of the project at risk. I ran an **early-access interview programme** and delivered a **redesign**.

<CaseSubsection title="Cross-team coordination">

To make the concept work, all products needed to **implement the app switcher** and **align visual design and branding**.

I coordinated closely with product managers and engineering leads and offered hands-on guidance to get it done.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="outcome">

<CaseSubsection title="One door into the suite">

The <a href="https://doc.sitecore.com/portal/en/developers/sitecore-cloud-portal/introduction-to-the-sitecore-cloud-portal.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Cloud Portal</a> is now the single point of entry to Sitecore’s product suite, the cornerstone of the unified digital experience platform strategy.

The switcher allowed users frictionless switching between apps.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="reflections">

<CaseSubsection title="Show, don’t ask">

Throughout the project, the portal was in perpetual identity crisis. We couldn’t nail down its primary focus and target audience, due to the many stakeholders and conflicting opinions.

I spent too much time asking the same strategy questions, to which the answers simply weren’t available.

When conversation stalls, pivot to showing designs — even when they’re probably wrong. Give people something tangible to react to. As a wise man once said:

<a href="https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/8668660-a-lot-of-times-people-don-t-know-what-they-want" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="italic">
  “A lot of times, people don’t know what they want until you show it to them.”
</a>

</CaseSubsection>
