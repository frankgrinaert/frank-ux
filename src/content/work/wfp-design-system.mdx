<CaseSection id="context">

<CaseSubsection title="UX at humanitarian scale">

The World Food Programme runs a **large portfolio of internal IT solutions**. Most are built and maintained by **external development partners**, with limited central UX oversight.

Without a design system teams actually use, every product reinvents interface patterns. Accessibility drifts, QA churn grows, and engineering time disappears into one-off styling.

The business stake is simple: **one credible foundation for quality and speed of delivery across the IT portfolio**.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="problem">

<CaseSubsection title="A system that worked against delivery">

The legacy design system was outdated, buried in tech debt, and painful to use and maintain. Worse: there was **no dedicated team** to evolve it.

Developers worked around it; hacks, duplicated components, slow releases.

Accessibility was inconsistent.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="approach">

<CaseSubsection title="Make the case to start fresh">

I led a formal audit and an internal presentation to spell out the cost of staying on the old foundation. Together with architects, I benchmarked modern frontend approaches and agreed: **reboot**, not incremental patch.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Zero to one">

I hired a frontend engineer and led a **zero-to-one** programme: a new code-first foundation shipped in roughly **two months**.

<CaseSubsection title="Ship code teams can trust">

The new system lives at <a href="https://designsystem.wfp.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">designsystem.wfp.org</a>: tokens, components, and documentation aimed at implementation in vendor codebases, not slide decks.

I evangelise internally, support pilot teams through migration, and keep architects aligned on an **AI-ready** stack — structured for AI-assisted development and, eventually, code prototypes from UX and Product without a full engineering loop.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Roll-out in progress">

This programme started a few months ago; adoption metrics are still forming. What is already true:

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Future-proof tech">

The design system is ideally suited to AI-assisted development. This can reduce dependency on Engineering, by enabling non-engineers to build interfaces autonomously.

<CaseSection id="reflections">

<CaseSubsection title="Read the room">

I underestimated sensitivities around the old system; sunk-cost attachment and pride in work that had clearly stopped scaling. I moved fast, delivered the new foundation in record time, and got the technical outcome. I also strained relationships by bulldozing past resistance instead of bringing more people along earlier.

The lesson parallels <a href="/work/blok">Blok</a>: you do not have a design system without reusable code developers trust; but at WFP I am learning that *how* you retire the old one matters as much as *what* you ship next.

</CaseSubsection>
