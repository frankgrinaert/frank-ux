Case study

Sitecore Cloud Portal

Designed and helped build the single entry point for Sitecore’s multi-product cloud suite — then used research to cut through homepage bloat and deliver what customers actually needed.

Sitecore Platform UX User research Design Lead

1 · Business problem

Customers could not navigate the platform we sold

After a wave of acquisitions, Sitecore’s value proposition was a suite — but the experience was a bag of separate products. Customers needed one place to enter the ecosystem and switch between apps, the way modern multi-product platforms (and buyers) expect.

Without that layer, expansion revenue and retention suffer: every login path is different, onboarding never compounds, and support costs climb. The Cloud Portal was the strategic answer — a unified front door and app switcher for the DXP.

2 · Product gap

Stakeholders wanted everything on the homepage

Early versions fell into “homepage syndrome” — every group lobbied for tiles, banners, and promos. The result overwhelmed users. My early-access interview programme showed people felt disoriented and overloaded; they could not find their next task.

The business risk was not polish — it was that the portal failed its job as the operating layer for the suite, undermining the entire unification narrative.

3 · Approach

Secure the mandate, then simplify with evidence

I pitched the CPO with a rapid concept for portal + app switcher and became Design Lead. When research contradicted the bloated direction, I presented findings and recommendations to secure PM buy-in, then paired closely with Engineering on the fix.

4 · Outcomes

What we shipped

A live Cloud Portal customers use as the suite entry — aligned with Blok for visual and component consistency. Qualitative outcome: users could orient and switch products without re-learning each app’s chrome.

Business
Platform story matches product reality — one brand, one door.
Product
Homepage scope tied to jobs-to-be-done, not stakeholder count.
Process
Research used to challenge leadership decisions — not only validate them.

5 · Reflection

Stakeholder management as a design skill

I learned to make ambiguous platform work tangible through iterative prototypes, and to use customer evidence when opinions diverge. Portal work also fed the design system: responsive, atomic improvements are documented in the mobile optimisation case study.
