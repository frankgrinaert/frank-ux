Case study

Blok Design System

After five acquisitions, Sitecore’s portfolio was a patchwork of UIs. Blok became the shared foundation — code, docs, and standards — that 12+ product teams adopted to ship faster with better UX.

Sitecore Design system React · NPM Principal Designer · DS Lead

1 · Business problem

Fragmentation was slowing the whole company

Sitecore had acquired multiple products with different visual languages, tech stacks, and quality bars. Customers experienced a disjointed platform; internally, every team paid a tax on custom UI and repeated accessibility mistakes.

Leadership needed cohesive UX at scale and engineering efficiency — not another PDF style guide. The design system had to be the lever: one implementation path, one quality baseline for usability, accessibility, and responsiveness.

Primary metric
12+ products onboarded to Blok
Secondary signal
Product development accelerated — less UI rework, faster feature delivery
My role
Owner from reboot through evangelisation, migration support, and hands-on code

2 · Product gap

The old system was not adopted

I inherited a fledgling system: a thin Figma library and a handful of code components, no dedicated team. Developer interviews were clear — the tools were hard to use, so teams skipped them. We were not getting quality or speed gains on paper or in production.

The user problem underneath: engineers and designers wasted cycles reconciling visuals per product; PMs could not rely on a predictable UI layer for roadmaps.

3 · Approach

Reboot, build, ship in code

I rebooted the programme with volunteer developers from across R&D, then learned enough React to finish the foundation myself. Blok shipped as:

  • React component library published to NPM
  • Documentation site — tokens, patterns, brand, Figma libraries, content guidelines
  • Standards engineering could enforce in review, not admire in Slack

Launches included R&D Town Hall and a Sitecore user conference — talk on YouTube, slides.

Roll-out meant working with PMs to prioritise adoption per product and pairing with engineers on migration — not throwing a Figma file over the wall.

4 · Outcomes

What changed for the business

Blok became the shared language between Design and Engineering. The measurable headline is adoption across 12+ teams. The operational outcome — reported back by engineers — was faster delivery with cleaner code and fewer UI defects.

“It saved us tons of time.” · “We were able to close a lot of QA tickets.” · “We can focus more on building functionality and providing value.”
  • “It's cool how quickly you can get results.”
  • “Lot less focus for us on making it look right.”
  • “Much cleaner and more maintainable code.”

5 · Reflection

Zero-to-one without a budget line

I proved I could run a zero-to-one initiative by generating internal momentum and attracting contributors — relationships and persistence matter as much as craft. I solidified my design-engineering practice: React, NPM publishing, and the belief that design systems are production systems, not decoration.
