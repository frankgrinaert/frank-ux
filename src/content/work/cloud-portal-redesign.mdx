<CaseSection id="context">

<CaseSubsection title="Identity crisis">

While building the <a href="/work/cloud-portal">Cloud Portal &amp; app switcher</a>, the suite finally had a front door — but the homepage was still taking shape in beta.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="problem">

<CaseSubsection title="Homepage bloat">

{/* The portal fell into [homepage syndrome](https://youtu.be/99e6d2purlo?t=300) */}

Many stakeholders and no single strategy on what the portal should be — app launcher, deployment tool, user admininistration, billboard, … ?

In the absence of focus, the answer became: ‘everything and nothing’.

The portal homepage had become cluttered with content, lacking hierarchy and direction. Early-access users felt overwhelmed and disoriented.

<img
  src="/work/portal-redesign-clutter.png"
  alt="Early Cloud Portal with heavy homepage content"
  className="w-full rounded-lg my-10"
/>

This was not a good first impression to make, as an aspiring SaaS platform.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="approach">

<CaseSubsection title="Course-correct with evidence">

To inform the way forward, **I interviewed early-access users**, synthesised findings, and presented them to the team: **users were overloaded**; prized features were invisible in practice.

The evidence secured buy-in for a significant simplification.

I translated the findings into a **streamlined redesign** of the portal & app switcher.

<FigmaEmbed
  src="https://www.figma.com/proto/t0NCQfaEOmsZQrmmjCGZaO?scaling=scale-down-width"
  title="Research readout and recommendations"
/>

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Deliver with care">

To ensure the redesign shipped as intended, I worked hand-in-hand with engineering on all the specifics: the dynamic recommendations system, responsiveness, and correct use of the <a href="/work/blok">design system</a>.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="reflections">

<CaseSubsection title="Debate with data">

Concrete customer evidence is more persuasive than theoretical arguments. Run it early, before opinions harden.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Share the why">

Engineers are often starved for business context and strongly appreciate knowing the ‘why’ behind a change.
