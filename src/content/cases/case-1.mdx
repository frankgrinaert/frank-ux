<CaseSubsection title="A fragmented product and organisation">

Sitecore wanted to expand its ageing enterprise CMS offering to a full digital experience platform; a category dominated by Adobe. To fill out its product suite, Sitecore acquired 6 companies, and started numerous zero-to-one products, all in under three years.

The result: Sitecore was a patchwork of smaller companies, distributed teams, on different tech stacks, working in isolation from each other.

<CaseSection id="problem">

<CaseSubsection title="Unify the experience">

The disjointed organization was mirrored in a disjointed user experience: a dozen different products, with no shared identity or design language. A far cry from the unified digital experience platform we envisaged.

Not to mention the wasted effort of designing and building the same UI patterns over and over again.

We needed to bring these islands together and create a cohesive and consistent user experience across the platform, all while reducing waste and accelerating development.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Design system">

I identified the design system as the highest-leverage problem I could solve; not a single product, but the building blocks for all products.

However, I had no budget, no team, and no formal mandate. I had to make the case and build momentum from scratch.

</CaseSubsection>
