Case study

AI authoring interaction design

Brought AI-assisted workflows into Sitecore’s headless CMS — where authors already work — instead of bolting on a disconnected “AI feature.”

Sitecore AI CMS

1 · Business problem

AI as table stakes for content platforms

Buyers expect generative assistance in content tools. Sitecore needed credible AI authoring in headless products to stay competitive — not a demo, but flows authors could trust in production.

The business stake: shorten content production cycles and reduce blank-page friction without breaking governance, brand, or existing component models.

2 · Product gap

AI that respects how authors work

Generic chat UIs do not map to structured content, fields, and approvals. The gap was interaction design: when AI suggests, when it edits in place, how authors review and accept output.

3 · Approach

Prototype in context

I designed and prototyped AI-assisted authoring inside realistic CMS screens — tied to components and tasks authors already perform.

AI authoring prototype (Figma) →

4 · Outcomes

Shipped direction for product

Delivered a concrete interaction model Engineering and PM could estimate and build — reducing ambiguity that often kills AI features in enterprise roadmaps. Connects to broader exploration in AI-first CMP concept.
