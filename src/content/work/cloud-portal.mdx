<CaseSection id="context">

<CaseSubsection title="Suite in sales, bag of apps in product">

After six acquisitions, Sitecore’s product organization was fragmented, <a href="/work/blok">as described in the Blok case study</a>. Yet to customers, Sitecore was positioned as a unified **digital experience platform**.

Buyers expect multi-product suites to behave like Adobe or Microsoft: **one front door, one identity, seamless switching**.

My internal framing of the challenge:

<FigmaEmbed src="https://embed.figma.com/proto/TKdfHvEfinvG1mdhr7AtpD?scaling=scale-down-width&embed-host=share" title="Design challenge" />

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="problem">

<CaseSubsection title="The platform was homeless">

Customers experienced the **opposite of a platform**: separate logins, choppy workflows between apps, and no way to discover sibling products.

Without a shared entry point, and without easy switching between apps, the platform story did not match the product reality.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="approach">

<CaseSubsection title="Secure the mandate">

I pitched a quick concept for a **unified portal and app launcher**, with an **app switcher** that would be embedded inside each product:

<img src="/work/portal-concept.png" alt="Rapid concept for a unified portal and app switcher" className="w-full rounded-lg border shadow-lg my-10" />

This got me the role of Design Lead on what would become the new **Cloud Portal**.

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Course correction">

Several months into development, feature creep was putting the success of the project at risk.

To remedy this, I ran an **early-access interview programme** and delivered a **redesign**:

<WorkCardEmbed id="cloud-portal-redesign" />

</CaseSubsection>

<CaseSubsection title="Build the platform layer">

We designed and shipped the portal and app switcher in tandem with the new <a href="/work/blok">Blok design system</a> — shared components, tokens, and patterns so the entry point felt like Sitecore, not another acquired skin.

I worked closely with engineering to align new and legacy products: one chrome, one switcher, one place to land after sign-in.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="outcome">

<CaseSubsection title="One door into the suite">

We shipped a live <a href="https://portal.sitecorecloud.io/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Cloud Portal</a> customers use as the suite entry — with an app switcher so they can move between products without re-learning each app’s chrome.

**Business:** The platform story matches product reality — one brand, one door.

**Product:** A single sign-in and a consistent shell across the portfolio.

Midway through delivery, the homepage itself needed a research-driven simplification. That story is in the <a href="/work/cloud-portal-redesign">Cloud Portal homepage redesign</a> case.

</CaseSubsection>

</CaseSection>

<CaseSection id="reflections">

<CaseSubsection title="Share the why">

Engineers are often starved for business context. Explaining *why* the portal and switcher mattered for the suite — not just *what* to build — made collaboration faster and decisions easier to align.

</CaseSubsection>
